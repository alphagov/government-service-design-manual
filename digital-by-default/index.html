All new or redesigned transactional services going live after
April 2014 must meet the Digital by Default service standard.

Each Digital by Default service is:

Usable

  • Able to meet the needs of its users
  • Designed for inclusion
  • Supported by a plan for assisting digital use
  • Device and platform independent
  • Seamlessly integrated with any non-digital steps
  • Part of GOV.UK in look, tone and feel
  • Easy to use

Agile

  • Owned by a single, skilled and empowered service manager
  • Built and operated by a multidisciplinary team
  • Delivered using an Agile methodology
  • Continuously improved based on user research and performance data
  • Able to support multivariant experiments
  • Tested with real users at the alpha and public beta phases
  • Able to be updated and improved on a daily basis
  • Tested in an environment identical to the live service

Effective

  • Achieving high levels of user satisfaction
  • Achieving a low cost per transaction
  • Showing a consistently high transaction success rate
  • Reaching high levels of digital take-up
  • Successfully tested by the minister responsible

Open

  • Publishing new, reusable code under an appropriate license
  • Using open standards and common platforms where they exist
  • Sharing progress and knowledge with stakeholders, colleagues and the public
  • Safe and secure
  • Built to an appropriate security level
