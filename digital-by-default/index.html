The new standard for government services

All new or redesigned transactional services going live after
April 2014 must meet the Digital by Default service standard.

Each Digital by Default service is:

Usable

  • Able to meet the needs of its users
  • Digital services so good that people prefer to use them
  • Accessible to those who can't use digital services
  • Device and platform independent
  • Seamlessly integrated with any non-digital steps
  • Part of GOV.UK in how it looks and feels
  • Has understood and challanged any existing policy or operational constraints

Agile

  • Owned by a single, skilled and empowered service manager
  • Built and operated by a multidisciplinary team
  • Delivered using an Agile methodology
  • Continuously improves based on user feedback and research
  • Can conduct multivariant experements on the live service
  • Is tried out with real users at the alpha and public beta phases
  • Can be updated and improved on a weekly basis.
  • Has a full end-to-end test environment.

Effective

  • Follows the GDS service design principles
  • Aims for and achieving high levels of user satisfaction
  • Achieves a low cost per transaction
  • Shows a high and consistent transaction success rate
  • Promotes and reaches higher levels of digital take-up
  • Has been used, end-to-end, successfully by the relevant minister

Open

  • Has code and content that is open and reusable
  • Uses open standards where they exist
  • Makes progress open and visible across government and the public
